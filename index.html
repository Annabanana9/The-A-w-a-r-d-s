For the Best Picture Award goes to the "Green Book" Directed by Peter Farrely.
Dr Don Shirley is a world-class African-American pianist, who is about to embark on a concert tour in the Deep South in 1962. In need of a driver and protection, Shirley recruits Tony Lip, a tough-talking bouncer from an Italian-American neighbourhood in the Bronx. Despite their differences, the two men soon develop an unexpected bond while confronting racism and danger in an era of segregation.

More honorable mentions: ROMA, A Star is Born, and VICE.

To Game of The Year award. It goes to Resident Evil 2 Remake.
With the drastic improvement of detail, smooth-storyline, and keeping consent with the rememberable, horror and action lore of Resident Evil 2 orignal. Fans of Xbox and PC notimate this game for this award.
"Capcom did a fantasic job of resurrecting all the best parts of the classic Resident Evil 2 and making them look, sound, and play like a 2019 game. If you're of the mind that the series had lost its way for a while there, this game is very much a return to form. The zombie combat is satisfying and exploring the dilapidated ruins of Raccoon City is a thrill. This establishes a new standard for remaking classic games."- Daemon Hatfield

Honorable Mentions: Blair Witch, Apex Legends, Mortal Kombat 11 and Medro Exodus

For the last award:

Most Annoying Public Stunt in 2019 Gaming History...
goes to...
Fornite

In Oct 14, 2019. Fornite's new chapter startled players when a blackhole devoured the whole Fortnite map. Leaving a blackhole in the middle of the screen. Players grew concerned when this lasted for hours. Most were worried if Epic Games cancelled Fornite after they took money from players. The internet exploded when this happen. Articles were published and Youtubers who made profit off of playing Fortnite, reported the stunt. Until Oct 15, where Epic Games deceived the players, the community, and the internet with a brand chapter and new map, along with brand new concept, and more lore. This is the reason why Fornite was awarded for the "Most Annoying Public Stunt in 2019 Gaming history".
